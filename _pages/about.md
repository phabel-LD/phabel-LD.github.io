I am a computational biologist and data scientist with a Licenciatura in Genomic Sciences from the Universidad Nacional Autónoma de México (UNAM). I am an author of 5 preprints (4 clinical, 1 computational) and the developer of **TMO**, a transformer-based framework for learning cell-state-dependent regulatory lags from single-cell multi-omic data.

I am passionate about interdisciplinary research, applying deep learning and statistical methods to solve problems in genomics, clinical research, and sports science. I have collaborated with clinicians at the ISSSTE Hospital Regional "General Ignacio Zaragoza" on multiple pilot randomised controlled trials in postoperative pain and septic shock.

My current research interests include:

- **Deep Learning for Genomics**: Transformers, attention mechanisms, single-cell multi-omics
- **Clinical Trial Statistics**: Linear mixed models, ROC analysis, logistic regression
- **Interdisciplinary Research**: Bridging computational biology and clinical medicine
- **Sports Science**: Physical training, biomechanics, performance analysis
